      Help — what does this mean?

      1. What is this dashboard?

      Real-time monitor for ESM6 (E-mini S&P 500 futures, June 2026 contract) at the 15-minute timeframe. The engine looks for reversal patterns using real order-flow data (who is buying vs selling each bar), then tracks how those patterns perform after they fire.

      2. What are R1, R2, R7?

      R1 — fade buyer dominance. Many buyers hit the bar but price drops anyway. Engine bets the drop continues — go short.
      R2 — fade seller dominance. Many sellers hit the bar but price rises. Engine bets the rise continues — go long.
      R7 — CVD divergence. Cumulative buy minus sell volume disagrees with bar direction over a 20-bar window. Engine bets price will revert toward the imbalance side.

      3. How to read the numbers

      • Wins X% of trades — hit rate. ~50% is a coin flip; >55% is meaningful.
      • Average reward +X R — average profit per trade, measured in units of one bar's volatility (ATR). +0.5R means a typical trade made half a "normal move" of profit.
      • Retention vs baseline — how live performance compares to the test-window calibration. 1.0 means matching; below 0.5 means significant decay.
      • Sample size — <30 trades is too small to judge. ≥30 is "first check"; ≥100 is "strong read"; ≥200 is "better read".
      • Live vs historical — historical = backfilled trades (already happened); live = real-time ingestion since the rules went live.

      4. Why do numbers move?

      • More trades arrive each minute → counts grow.
      • Market regime changes (e.g. trending vs choppy) shift mean R and hit rate.
      • Extending the historical backfill window grows the historical sample and can dilute or strengthen aggregates.
      • Live SDK gaps (e.g. Databento upstream issue) freeze the live-only counts even though historical numbers don't change.

      5. What to do

      SituationAction
      live-only sample < 60 wait — sample too small for verdict
      mean_r turns negative on n ≥ 30 investigate the affected rule one at a time
      hit rate drops below 50% check Recent performance card; flag a session if one is dragging
      live tail stale > 30 min restart Flask or check Databento
      retention < 0.5 but mean_r still positive continue monitoring, no action

      6. Emergency revert

      Revert real-flow rule path to the original conservative thresholds: set OF_REAL_THRESHOLDS_ENABLED=0 in .env and restart Flask. All bars revert to the proxy-tuned thresholds. No data is lost; the live tail and historical outcomes JSONL are unaffected.
